    THE PET TRACKER 🔎 🐾

    How does this application work?

    • You can create an account and be able to register a lost or a rescued pet (cat or dog).
    • You can search for a match of your lost pet in the list of registered rescued pets.
    • Instead, you can look for a coincidence of a rescued pet in the list of registered lost animals.
    • The search criteria are based on the following characteristics:
      • Breed. One word must match.
      • Color. One color o more should be the same.
      • Distance. We consider a distance of 30 miles around the locations where the pet was lost and rescued.

    Did you know?

    About 92% of lost dogs have a family.

    I lost my pet, what do I need to do?

      We propose next steps:
    • Check local shelters' websites every 1-3 days for new arrivals.
    • Check and post to the many lost pet Facebook pages for your area.
    • If your dog has a microchip, contact your microchip company and ensure your phone number is updated in their system.
    • Create a user on this website and check the results every one to three days.
    • If you have found a match that may be your pet, contact the rescue person.

    I have rescued a pet, what should I do?

    • If you found a lost pet, the best thing that you can do is hold onto the animal and look for the owner.
    • If the rescued pet is wearing a tag with the phone number, call and send text.
    • Take the pet to a shelter or veterinarian to scan for a microchip. Ask for the microchip number and search it on the Microchip look-up website.
    • If you are unable to care for the animal, you may contact your local shelter.
    • Look for the owner by doing the recomendations when you have lost your pet.

    WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW: Pets are considered to be the legal property of their owners. Finding a pet does not entitle the finder to keep, sell, or give away the pet, nor does it convey ownership to the finder. If you find a pet, you must actively look for the owner for at least 90 days. Even after that period of time has elapsed, the owner may still have the right to reclaim his/her pet under certain circumstances.
